What is Past Perfect Continuous?

The Past Perfect Continuous (also called "had been + -ing") shows an action that was happening for a period of time before another past action. Think: someone had been doing something, and then something else happened.

Positive: S + had been + V-ing.
Negative: S + had not been + V-ing.

Quick difference (easy)

Simple Past: one finished action (I ate dinner).
Past Continuous: action in progress (I was eating dinner at 7pm).
Past Perfect: action completed before another past action (I had eaten before he arrived).
Past Perfect Continuous: action that was ongoing for some time before another past action (I had been eating for 30 minutes before he arrived).

âœ… Tip: Use Past Perfect Continuous when you want to show duration of a past action before another past action. Look for words like for, since, all day, before.
