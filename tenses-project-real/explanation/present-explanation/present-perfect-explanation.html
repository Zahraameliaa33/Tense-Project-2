What is Present Perfect Tense?

The Present Perfect Tense connects past actions with the present. It is used to describe experiences, changes, or results of actions that affect the current situation.

In Present Perfect, have and has carry the present time connection. The main verb always takes the past participle (V3) form. You never need to worry about regular or irregular verbs just use their V3 form. This tense shows an action that has a link to the present moment.

Quick difference (easy)

Simple Present: one regular or general action (I eat dinner).
Present Continuous: action in progress (I am eating dinner).
Present Perfect: action completed before now with present relevance (I have eaten).
Present Perfect Continuous: action that has been ongoing up to now (I have been eating for 30 minutes).
