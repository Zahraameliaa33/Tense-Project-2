What is Simple Tense?

The Simple Tense is used to describe actions that happen regularly, happened in the past, or will happen in the future. It shows the action clearly without extra focus on progress or completion.

Positive: Just use the verb form: V1 (present), V2 (past), or will + V1 (future).
Negative: Add do/does/did + not + base verb.
Question: Use Do/Does/Did + subject + base verb?

Quick difference (easy)

Simple Present: one regular or general action (I eat dinner).
Present Continuous: action in progress (I am eating dinner).
Present Perfect: action completed before now with present relevance (I have eaten).
Present Perfect Continuous: action that has been ongoing up to now (I have been eating for 30 minutes).
