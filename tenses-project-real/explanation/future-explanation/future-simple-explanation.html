Future Simple expresses actions or events that will happen later than now. It is often used for promises, predictions, spontaneous decisions, or future facts.

Quick difference (easy)

Simple Future: one action that will happen (I will eat dinner).
Future Continuous: action that will be in progress (I will be eating dinner at 7pm).
Future Perfect: action completed before another future moment (I will have eaten before he arrives).
Future Perfect Continuous: action that will have been ongoing for some time before another future moment (I will have been eating for 30 minutes before he arrives).
